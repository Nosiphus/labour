2020 Year In Review - Lunatics with Sean Crain

Monday, December 28, 2020 - 2020 In Review - Sean Crain

Well, here we are once again, with less than a week remaining until an old year fades away and a new year begins. As is custom here, I will begin my year in review.

2020 has been a dumpster fire no matter how you look at it. The coronavirus pandemic has killed hundreds of thousands of people in the United States alone, the economy is in the worst shape it has been in since the Great Depression, racial injustice is back at the forefront of debate, massive wildfires and unusually strong tropical hurricanes have made it clear that the climate crisis is continuing to worsen, people are at each other’s throats over the results of the election, and an outgoing president who refuses to accept his fate continues to fan the flames of outrage among his supporters.

There’s no honest way to talk about this year without including all of its misery and the destructiveness that has occurred during it. Make no doubt, 2020 is the single worst year of not just the last ten years, but also the most turbulent since 1968, over fifty years ago.

This year, I decided that the best way to encapsulate what happened throughout the past twelve months was through first-hand experiences.

When George Floyd was killed, what was your reaction, and how did you feel about the following protests?
My reaction was that, while the officer was in the wrong, everyone was blowing it out of proportion. I know you may not agree with me, but people should not hold up one man's death, a criminal's death, as a symbol for a political/racial/social movement. While there is still racial injustice in the world, we cannot rid ourselves of it completely, nor should we hold an entire group of people responsible for the death of a man when it was one individual's mistake. The riots, not protests, were stupid. I understand you want to get your point across, but violence and criminal activity is not the way to do it. I know some were protesting peacefully, but the media tended to only cover the portion that grabs attention. I think that the George Floyd thing blew up for no good reason, and that if it weren't for BLM looking for a crutch to get the government to bottlefeed them, it would've been talked about for about a week at most, then forgotten.

How do you think the government handled the pandemic and the protests?
The government did what they could. I understand that some people wanted to stay locked up for safety, but the majority of Americans can't survive like that. Economically, we just couldn't survive like that. I think that the government could've done better, but I understand that their attention was on more important matters, the pandemic. Honestly, in Portland and Seattle I would've sent in the National Guard as soon as the riots started to help prevent damages and losses. Many innocent people lost their livelihoods just because a bunch of selfish "protestors" wanted to get their point across.

How concerning were the Australia and California wildfires?
California is on fire every year. It didn't bother me too much, but I was worried for people's safety. It is going to keep happening until they actually thin the forests and do proper conservation to prevent them. In Australia, I was a bit more worried because it is less frequent and the number of endangered species there is significantly larger.

How did you feel about the outcome of the election?
I do not agree with the outcome of the election. I think there is obvious fraud and corruption going on, especially within the Democratic Party, but this is nothing new to me. Biden may be inaugurated in January, but I won't be happy about it. I won't protest it, however, like the Democrats did when Trump won fair and square. I also won't be surprised when Kamala Harris is sworn in because Joe Biden either died or was deemed unfit to be president. I think there should be more investigation on the election personally. We spent three years looking into fake Russia stuff, surely we can spend another year looking into the fudging of laws and regulations going on into the political parties and the polls.

Are you planning on getting vaccinated as soon as possible, or are you planning on waiting for a while?
Yes, I plan to get the vaccine as soon as I can. I am at risk due to my various health issues, one of them being asthma.

Lastly, what are your hopes for 2021?
My hopes for 2021 is that we get back to a somewhat "normal" life, that the new heads of our country don't run it into the ground, and that I can enjoy getting together in large groups with friends and family again. Also, I want in person classes back, I hate online class. I can't learn well this way.
